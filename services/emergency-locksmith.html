Emergency Lockout Tips

What to do while waiting for our emergency locksmith

Lockouts happen to everyone, and panicking can make the situation feel much worse. Take a few deep breaths and remind yourself that help is on the way. Avoid attempting to force entry, as this can cause unnecessary damage to your door, frame, or lock, leading to costly repairs. Instead, focus on staying safe and keeping a clear head so you can communicate effectively with your locksmith when they arrive. If you have children, pets, or vulnerable people with you, reassure them and keep them calm as well.

Carefully walk around your property and check for any other doors or windows that may have been left unlocked. Sometimes, a side gate, back door, or even a garage entrance can provide safe access. However, only attempt to enter through points that are easily accessible and do not require you to climb or force anything open, as this could result in injury or damage. If you do find an open entry, ensure it is safe and secure before entering, and let your locksmith know so they can cancel the call if no longer needed.

If the weather is poor or you feel unsafe waiting outside, look for a nearby place to stay warm and dry. This could be a neighbour’s house, a local shop, or even your car if it’s accessible. Let your locksmith know where you’ll be waiting so they can find you easily when they arrive. If you have children or elderly people with you, it’s especially important to keep them comfortable and protected from the elements. Always keep your phone handy so you can stay in contact with your locksmith and receive updates on their arrival time.

For your security and to comply with legal requirements, locksmiths will usually ask for proof that you live at or have permission to access the property. While you wait, locate a form of identification such as a driver’s licence, utility bill, or tenancy agreement that shows your name and address. Having this ready will speed up the process once your locksmith arrives and ensures that your property remains secure from unauthorised access. If you don’t have ID on you, let your locksmith know in advance so they can advise on alternative verification methods.
